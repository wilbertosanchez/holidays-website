chinese new year food

During Chinese New Year, popular foods include dumplings, symbolizing wealth, and fish, representing abundance and prosperity. Other favorites are spring rolls for wealth, nian gao for growth, and sweet rice balls for family unity.

chinese new year activites

Traditional Chinese New Year activities include setting off fireworks to drive away evil spirits and decorating homes with red lanterns and couplets for good luck. Families also gather for reunions, exchange red envelopes (hongbao) with money, and partake in lion and dragon dances to bring prosperity and happiness

chinese new year orgin

The origin of Chinese New Year dates back over 4,000 years to the Shang Dynasty, where it was originally a celebration to honor deities and ancestors and mark the end of the winter season. It is closely tied to the lunar calendar and the myth of Nian, a monster that would terrorize villages, and people would use loud noises, fire, and red decorations to scare it away, which eventually became part of the festive traditions.
