hanukkah food

Hanukkah foods, like latkes (potato pancakes) and sufganiyot (jelly doughnuts), are fried in oil to commemorate the miracle of the oil. Other traditional dishes include brisket, kugel, and tzimmes, creating a festive and delicious holiday meal.

hanukkah activities

During Hanukkah, families light the menorah, adding one candle each night to celebrate the miracle of the oil. Other fun activities include playing dreidel, exchanging gifts, and enjoying festive meals together.

hanukkah origin

Hanukkah, also known as the Festival of Lights, commemorates the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem after the Jewish people defeated the Seleucid Empire in 165 BCE. The miracle of Hanukkah is the story of a small amount of oil, enough for only one day, that burned for eight days in the temple's menorah.
