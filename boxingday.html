boxing day food

Boxing Day, traditionally celebrated the day after Christmas, features a variety of foods, often centered around leftovers from the Christmas feast. Some common dishes include cold cuts, roast meats (like turkey or ham), mince pies, Christmas pudding, and a variety of sandwiches made with leftover meats and vegetables.

boxing day activities

Boxing Day activities often include shopping, as many stores have big sales, or spending time with family and friends. People also enjoy outdoor activities like walking, watching sports (especially football or rugby), and relaxing at home with festive leftovers.

boxing day origin

Boxing Day originated in the United Kingdom and is traditionally celebrated the day after Christmas. The name comes from the practice of giving "Christmas boxes" – gifts or donations – to those in service professions, such as postal workers and tradespeople, as a token of appreciation for their work throughout the year.
