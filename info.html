Everything You Need To Know About The Holiday

chinese newyear

Chinese New Year is a vibrant celebration full of joy, with families coming together to enjoy delicious food, fireworks, and colorful traditions. It's a time to welcome good fortune, honor ancestors, and share love and happiness for the year ahead.

Dragobete

Dragobete is a Romanian holiday celebrated on February 24th, honoring love and the arrival of spring. It is marked by young couples exchanging gifts, flowers, and kisses, symbolizing the renewal of love and nature.

diwali

Diwali is a lively festival of lights, where families decorate their homes with diyas and celebrate with sweets, firecrackers, and prayers. It symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is a time for joy, togetherness, and new beginnings.

Hanukkah

Hanukkah is a joyous Jewish festival that celebrates the miracle of the oil lasting eight days in the ancient temple, symbolized by the lighting of the menorah. It's a time for family gatherings, exchanging gifts, playing dreidel, and enjoying delicious foods like latkes and sufganiyot.

Pingxi Lantern Festival

The Pingxi Lantern Festival, held in Taiwan on the 15th day of the Lunar New Year, features thousands of colorful lanterns released into the sky. It symbolizes letting go of past misfortunes and making wishes for the future

Boxing Day

Boxing Day, celebrated on December 26th, is a holiday traditionally observed in the UK, Canada, and other Commonwealth nations. It is a day for giving to the less fortunate, often marked by sales and sporting events.

Dia de los Muertos

Día de los Muertos is a colorful and heartfelt Mexican celebration that honors deceased loved ones with altars, marigolds, and offerings. It’s a time to remember and celebrate the lives of those who’ve passed, embracing both joy and remembrance.

St. Patrick’s Day

St. Patrick’s Day, celebrated on March 17th, honors St. Patrick, the patron saint of Ireland. It is marked by parades, wearing green, and celebrating Irish culture with music, dancing, and traditional foods.

Holi

Holi, known as the Festival of Colors, is a vibrant Hindu celebration that marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. Participants throw colored powders at each other, dance, sing, and enjoy festive foods with friends and family.
