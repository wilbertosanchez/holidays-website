diwali food

Diwali food includes a variety of sweets like ladoos, barfis, and gulab jamun, symbolizing the sweetness of life and the festival's joy. Savory snacks like samosas, pakoras, and chivda are also popular, offering a perfect balance to the festive spread.

diwali activities

Diwali is a time for celebration, with activities such as decorating homes with lights, performing prayers, and enjoying delicious sweets with family and friends. It's also a season of giving, where people exchange gifts, play games, and share joy with those in need.

diwali origin

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, originates from ancient India and is celebrated to mark the triumph of light over darkness and good over evil. It is traditionally associated with the return of Lord Rama to his kingdom after 14 years of exile, during which he defeated the demon king Ravana, as described in the Hindu epic, the Ramayana.
